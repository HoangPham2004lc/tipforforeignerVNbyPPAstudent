Transportation
Grab (Ride-hailing)
Use Grab app for safe, reliable rides. Always confirm license plate and driver details. Grab Bike is cheapest, Grab Car for comfort.
Motorbike Taxis (Xe Om)
Negotiate price before riding. Always wear helmet. Hold onto the driver or grab rails. Agree on price in Vietnamese Dong.
Buses
Local buses are very cheap but crowded. Download bus apps or ask locals for routes. Have exact change ready.
Taxis
Use reputable companies like Mai Linh or Vinasun. Insist on using the meter. Avoid unmarked taxis at tourist areas.

Food & Dining
Street Food
Look for busy stalls with high turnover. Eat where locals eat. Avoid pre-cut fruits and ice from unknown sources initially.
Restaurant Etiquette
Tipping is not mandatory but appreciated (5-10%). Share dishes family-style. Use chopsticks properly or ask for fork/spoon.
Must-Try Dishes
Pho (noodle soup), Banh Mi (sandwiches), Bun Cha (grilled pork), Cao Lau (Hoi An noodles), Vietnamese coffee.

Culture & Etiquette
Greetings
Slight bow or nod is respectful. Handshakes are common in business. Use both hands when giving/receiving business cards.
Temple/Pagoda Visits
Remove shoes before entering. Dress modestly. Don't point feet toward altars. Small donation in money box is appreciated.
Photography
Ask permission before photographing people. Avoid military/government buildings. Be respectful at religious sites.
Bargaining
Expected at markets, not in stores with fixed prices. Start at 30-50% of asking price. Smile and be friendly.
Gift Giving
Avoid giving even numbers of flowers. Use both hands. Gifts are usually opened later, not immediately.

Money & Payments
Currency
Vietnamese Dong (VND) is the local currency. USD sometimes accepted at tourist areas but change given in VND.
ATMs
Widely available. BIDV, Vietcombank, and Sacombank ATMs are reliable. Check your bank's international fees.
Cash vs Cards
Cash is king for street food and local shops. Cards accepted at malls, hotels, upscale restaurants. Always carry cash.
Mobile Payments
Momo, ZaloPay popular locally. International cards work with these apps. QR codes common for payments.

üí° Money Tip

Keep small bills (10,000, 20,000, 50,000 VND) for street vendors and tips. Large bills can be hard to break.

Safety & Health
General Safety
Vietnam is generally safe for tourists. Petty theft can occur - keep valuables secure. Avoid displaying expensive items.
Scams to Avoid
Overcharging foreigners, fake gold shops, motorbike rental scams, inflated taxi fares. Research common scams beforehand.
Health Precautions
Drink bottled water. Avoid ice initially. Bring sunscreen and insect repellent. Consider travel insurance.
